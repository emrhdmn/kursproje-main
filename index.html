The Zero Point of History; Göbeklitepe

Located in the Germuş mountains of Southeastern Anatolia, this property offers monumental round-oval and rectangular megalithic structures built by hunter-gatherers in the Decrepit Neolithic Period between 9,600 and 8,200 BC. These monuments were probably used in connection with rituals related to funeral ceremonies. The distinctive T-shaped columns are carved with wild animal paintings that give an insight into the lifestyles and beliefs of the people who lived in Upper Mesopotamia about 11,500 years ago. MESOPOTAMIA

Outstanding Universal Value

HUMAN

The monumental buildings in Göbeklitepe show the creative human genius of these early societies.

LOCATION

It is located in upper Mesopotamia, a region where the oldest agricultural communities in the world originated.

MONUMENT

The monuments were probably used in connection with social events and rituals, and some of them have T-shaped columns of limestone, with a height of up to 5.50 meters.

CRAFTS

There are also a large number of decorative elements and handicraft works that give an idea of the lifestyle of the societies occupying the space.

ARCHITECTURE

It testifies to innovative construction techniques, including the integration of frequently decorated T-shaped limestone columns, which also perform architectural functions.
